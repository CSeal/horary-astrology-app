# Competitor Research — Horary Astrology Mobile App

## Research Scope

Analysis covers: direct horary competitors, indirect general-astrology apps with relevant overlap, and professional desktop tools that define the ceiling of practitioner expectations. All data reflects publicly available information as of Q1–Q2 2026.

---

## Market Overview

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Global astrology app market size (2026) | $6.27B |
| Projected market size (2035) | ~$49B |
| CAGR | ~20.2% |
| Typical free-to-paid conversion (astrology apps) | 3–8% |
| Dominant platform | iOS (60%+ of astrology app revenue) |
| Fastest-growing segment | AI-powered interpretation + personalization |

The astrology app market is in a sustained growth phase driven by Gen Z / millennial interest in spiritual self-care and decision-support tools. No dominant player exists specifically for horary astrology — the niche is technically underserved at the mobile consumer level.

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|

| Price | $15.99 upfront (one-time purchase) |
| Rating | 4.38 / 5 (63 ratings — very small install base) |
| Target user | Practicing astrologers |
| House system | Regiomontanus |

**Strengths**:
- Full horary technique implementation
- Regiomontanus houses (correct traditional method)

**Weaknesses**:
- Complex UI designed for practitioners; zero beginner onboarding
- No AI-generated plain-language interpretation

- UI design dated (pre-iOS 16 aesthetic)

**Positioning gap**: This is the closest direct competitor but serves only the practitioner segment. A beginner would abandon it within minutes.

## Key Market Gaps

### Gap 1: No Beautiful Mobile-First Horary App
The best horary tools (Janus, Solar Fire, Morinus) are desktop or web-only. The one mobile horary app (Horary Chart) has a complex practitioner UI with no beginner path. **This is the primary opportunity.**

### Gap 2: No Beginner-to-Practitioner Progression
All apps are either too simple (Co-Star — entertainment only) or too complex (Astro Gold, Janus — assumes expertise). No app guides a curious beginner through real horary technique at their own pace.

### Gap 3: AI + Traditional Method Hybrid is Underserved
Co-Star uses AI for personality text but not real predictive technique. No app combines William Lilly horary methodology with AI-generated plain-language interpretation. **This hybrid is our technical differentiator.**

### Gap 4: No Freemium Horary Option
Every horary app uses upfront payment ($15.99–$300). Freemium (5 free questions/month) removes acquisition friction while the question limit creates natural conversion pressure.

### Gap 5: Latency Gap — No Instant Human-Level Answer
Lunaton (best human service) requires 24h. Our app answers in under 2 seconds. For time-sensitive decisions, this is a decisive advantage.

### Gap 6: Russian-Speaking Market Underserved
Lunaton has some Russian presence but no Russian UI. All other competitors are English-only. Astrology is deeply embedded in Russian-speaking culture; a native Russian-language horary app has minimal direct competition.

## Positioning Strategy

### vs. Lunaton
**Message**: "Instant AI interpretation vs. 24-hour human wait. Same traditional technique, 1000x faster."
**Target**: Lunaton users who want faster answers; Russian-speaking market.

### vs. Horary Chart App
**Message**: "Beautiful beginner UX vs. complex practitioner interface. Real horary, without the learning curve."
**Target**: Curious beginners who tried Horary Chart and bounced.

### vs. Co-Star
**Message**: "Real horary technique vs. entertainment astrology. Ask a specific question. Get a real answer."
**Target**: Co-Star users who have outgrown personality descriptions and want predictive capability.

### vs. Janus / Solar Fire (Practitioners)
**Message**: "Your horary tool, now on your phone. Regiomontanus houses, William Lilly dignities, always with you."
**Target**: Professional astrologers who use desktop tools and have no mobile equivalent.
