# ASO Brief — AstraSk Horary Astrology App

**Date**: 2026-06-04
**Stage**: StageM1-MarketResearch
**Purpose**: App Store Optimization metadata, keyword strategy, description copy, and screenshot brief for the AstraSk MVP launch on iOS App Store and Google Play.

**Critical constraints (from aso-virality-brief.md)**:
- App title: max 30 characters including spaces
- Subtitle: max 30 characters including spaces
- Keyword field: max 100 characters, comma-no-space format, ZERO repetition with title/subtitle words
- Apple Guideline 4.3(b): "fortune telling" category — must demonstrate unique, high-quality experience in App Review submission
- Screenshots MUST show actual app functionality, not splash screens or logo art
- Each App Store locale (en-US, ru) gets separate 30+30+100 allocation — both must be populated

## Section 1: App Name Candidates

Five candidates optimized for: keyword density (primary search terms), brand clarity, Apple 4.3(b) differentiation, and Russian-market relevance.

**Character counts are confirmed against the 30-character limit for both Name and Subtitle fields.**

---

### Candidate A — Recommended Primary

**Name**: AstraSk: Horary Chart
**Character count**: 22 chars

**Subtitle**: Ask. Get an Instant Answer.
**Character count**: 28 chars

**Keyword analysis**:
- "Horary Chart" in the name directly captures the top-performing niche keyword with near-zero competition
- "Ask" mirrors the use case verb users type in search
- "Instant Answer" captures the speed differentiator and the "yes no answer" search intent cluster
- The brand name AstraSk is short, distinct, and brand-safe

**Competitor differentiation note**: No competitor uses "Horary Chart" in their name with an accessible subtitle. The existing "Astrology: Horary Chart" app (price $23.99) uses a dry functional subtitle "Horary astrology." Our subtitle leads with the user benefit (instant answer), not the feature name.

**Apple 4.3(b) signal**: "Horary Chart" frames the app as a chart calculation tool, not a fortune-telling service — this is the correct framing per the ASO brief's compliance analysis.

---

### Candidate B — Broader Discovery

**Name**: AstraSk: Ask the Stars
**Character count**: 22 chars

**Subtitle**: Horary Chart & AI Verdict
**Character count**: 26 chars

**Keyword analysis**:
- "Ask the Stars" is an emotional trigger phrase with low competition in the store
- "Horary Chart" moves to subtitle (still indexed) — frees the name for brand clarity
- "AI Verdict" signals the AI interpretation differentiator and the yes/no output format

**Competitor differentiation note**: Co-Star's premium feature uses "Ask the stars" language, meaning this framing has proven user resonance. AstraSk's use of it in the name claims the phrase in the horary context.

---

### Candidate C — AI-Positioning Emphasis

**Name**: AstraSk: Astrology AI
**Character count**: 22 chars

**Subtitle**: Horary Chart, Real Answers
**Character count**: 26 chars

**Keyword analysis**:
- "Astrology AI" captures the growing "AI astrology" search cluster
- "Horary Chart" anchors the technical differentiator in subtitle
- "Real Answers" counters the dominant competitor pain point (generic, vague interpretations)

**Risk**: "Astrology AI" is a competitive keyword cluster (Nummi, Co-Star, Nebula all compete there). Recommend only if Russian-market launch is secondary to English ASO.

---

### Candidate D — Question-First Positioning

**Name**: AstraSk — Horary Question
**Character count**: 26 chars

**Subtitle**: Classical Chart. AI Insight.
**Character count**: 29 chars

**Keyword analysis**:
- "Horary Question" targets the "astrology question answer" search cluster
- "Classical Chart" signals traditional method differentiation from entertainment apps
- "AI Insight" completes the value chain: traditional technique + modern AI

**Competitor differentiation note**: The word "Classical" directly triggers the 4.3(b) differentiation — Apple reviewers can see this is not a generic horoscope app.

---

### Candidate E — Minimal / Brand-Led

**Name**: AstraSk
**Character count**: 7 chars

**Subtitle**: Horary Astrology, AI Answer
**Character count**: 27 chars

**Keyword analysis**:
- Maximally clean brand name — easy to type, share, and remember
- Full keyword load moves to subtitle: "Horary Astrology" + "AI Answer" covers both primary terms
- Leaves name entirely keyword-field-free (no wasted characters in title)

**Risk**: Without a descriptive word in the name, discovery for cold users is weaker. Best suited if strong community / influencer launch drives direct brand-name searches from day one.

---

### Recommendation

**Launch with Candidate A** ("AstraSk: Horary Chart" / "Ask. Get an Instant Answer.").

Rationale: Balances keyword specificity (horary chart = lowest competition, high relevance), benefit clarity (instant answer = top competitor pain point = top search intent), and brand recognition. If ASO data after 30 days shows weak conversion on "Horary Chart" searches (low volume), iterate to Candidate B.

```
Ask a specific question. Get a real answer — in seconds.

AstraSk uses the classical horary astrology method (William Lilly, Regiomontanus houses) to cast a chart the moment you ask your question and interpret it with AI in plain language. No birth time required. No astrology knowledge needed.

HOW IT WORKS

1. Type your sincere, specific question
2. The app captures the exact moment and your location
3. A horary chart is calculated using traditional technique (Swiss Ephemeris DE431)
4. AI interprets the chart's significators and delivers a clear verdict: YES, NO, MAYBE, or UNCLEAR
5. Save the reading to your question journal

This is horary astrology — a 600-year-old predictive technique used by astrologers since William Lilly's "Christian Astrology" (1647). Your question is answered by analyzing the chart cast for the precise moment it was asked, not by a daily sun-sign horoscope.

WHAT YOU GET

- Instant verdict: YES / NO / MAYBE / UNCLEAR with confidence level
- AI-generated interpretation in plain language — no astrology knowledge required
- Planetary significators: the planets that represent you and your question's subject
- Void-of-course Moon warning when applicable
- Question journal: all your readings saved locally on your device
- Full English and Russian interface

WHY IT'S DIFFERENT

Every other horary app shows you a raw chart and expects you to interpret it yourself. AstraSk interprets it for you — using the same classical rulebook (essential dignities, house-based significators, reception) that a trained astrologer would use.

Every other horary app costs $15–$300 upfront. AstraSk is free to try — 5 questions per month, no commitment.

Other horary tools (Morinus, Janus, Solar Fire) are web-only or Windows-only. AstraSk is the first beautiful, native mobile horary app built for both iOS and Android.

- Will this relationship work out?
- Should I accept the job offer?
- Will I get the money I'm owed?
- Is this the right time to move?
- Will my health situation improve?

Any sincere, specific yes/no question about a real situation in your life.

WHAT HORARY IS NOT

Horary astrology is not a sun-sign horoscope. It does not predict your personality. It answers one specific question by analyzing the planetary positions at the exact moment the question was asked. The technique was developed and codified by William Lilly in 17th-century England and remains the most rigorous predictive method in traditional Western astrology.

All readings are stored locally on your device. No account required. No personal data collected beyond the question text, your location (used only for chart calculation), and the timestamp. Location is used only at the moment of the question and is never stored or shared.
```

## Section 4: Screenshots Brief

**Format**: 5 screenshots for iOS (6.9-inch required for 2026 submissions per Apple guidelines). Same content adapted for Play Store.

**Core principle**: First 3 screenshots carry ~70% of conversion weight. Lead with differentiators, not features. Every screenshot needs a clear caption (Apple OCR indexes screenshot text).

**Visual constraints**: dark celestial background (per design system), Cormorant Garamond for headers, Inter for body, accent gold (#F5C842), YES green (#22D3A4), NO red (#F87171).

**Apple 4.3(b) compliance**: screenshots MUST show actual chart functionality — verdict display, significators, AI interpretation — not splash screens or abstract art.

---

### Screenshot 1 — The Core Value Promise (conversion-critical)

**Goal**: Capture the user's primary search intent ("yes no astrology", "instant answer", "ask astrology question") in a single glance.

**What to show**:
- A completed verdict card showing a real question (e.g., "Will I get the job offer?")
- The verdict badge prominently displayed (YES in green, large)
- The confidence band (HIGH)
- One sentence of the AI interpretation visible below
- The AstraSk app name/wordmark at top

**Caption text** (large, high contrast, Apple OCR indexed):
"Ask your question. Get an instant answer."

**Why this works**: Immediately communicates the differentiator (instant answer, real question, real verdict) in the 7-second user scan window. Outperforms competitors whose screenshot 1 shows a natal chart or app logo.

---

### Screenshot 2 — The Differentiator: AI Interpretation

**Goal**: Show what no competitor can show — the AI plain-language explanation.

**What to show**:
- The full VerdictCard component: judgment badge + confidence + AI interpretation paragraph (4-6 sentences visible)
- The significators section (showing "You — Mercury in Gemini, 7th house" type rows)
- A dark, beautiful layout — premium feel

**Caption text**:
"AI explains what the chart means. In plain language."

**Why this works**: Directly addresses the top competitor pain point ("confusing / no explanation"). This screenshot is the proof-of-differentiator. A user who bounced from Horary Chart because it was confusing sees immediately that AstraSk is different.

---

### Screenshot 3 — The Question Input (instant / low friction)

**Goal**: Show how easy it is to start — counter the "complex / confusing" complaint about competitors.

**What to show**:
- The AskForm component: the text input with a sample question typed in ("Will my business partnership succeed?")
- The elegant question input UI
- Location indicator showing "Paris, France" or similar (showing automatic detection works)
- The "Ask" button styled prominently in accent gold

**Caption text**:
"Just type your question. The app does the rest."

**Why this works**: Visualizes the zero-friction entry point. No birth date, no complex setup. Counter-programs the "complicated" reputation of horary apps.

---

### Screenshot 4 — The Journal (retention / trust)

**Goal**: Show the journal feature — differentiator over Horary Chart (iOS) and most general horoscope apps. Signals long-term value.

**What to show**:
- The Journal tab with 3-4 entries visible
- Each entry showing: question text, date, verdict badge (YES/NO/MAYBE)
- A clean, scannable list with the dark celestial card design

**Caption text**:
"Every reading saved. Your question history in one place."

**Why this works**: Addresses the feature request "save readings / history" (found in Co-Star, AstroClub, and Horary Astrology Pro user feedback). Also signals the app has value over time — not just a one-shot tool.

---

### Screenshot 5 — Language + Trust (Russian market + social proof)

**Goal**: Capture Russian-speaking users. Also: show the bilingual capability, which is unique among all horary apps.

**What to show**:
- Split-screen or side-by-side showing the same verdict screen in both English and Russian
- OR: A Russian-language question in the input ("Примут ли меня на работу?") with a Russian verdict response
- The language toggle in settings visible
- Caption in both languages

**Caption text** (bilingual):
"Full Russian + English interface. Хорарная астрология, доступная каждому."
(Translation: "Horary astrology, accessible to everyone.")

**Why this works**: Russian-market users who see a Russian App Store listing AND Russian screenshots convert at significantly higher rates. The Russian subtitle "доступная каждому" (accessible to everyone) directly counters the "confusing" competitor pain point.

**Alternative for Screenshot 5 if split-screen is too complex**: Show the Settings screen with the EN/RU toggle, monthly question counter, and the "5 free questions per month" indicator — converting the freemium model into a visual trust signal.
